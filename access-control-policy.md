# Access Control Policy

## Purpose

This policy establishes the requirements for controlling access to the information systems and information assets of EduTrack Learning Solutions. It is designed to ensure that access is granted only to authorized individuals based on legitimate business needs while supporting the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of organizational information.

## Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, consultants, interns, third-party service providers, and any individual who accesses the organization's systems, networks, cloud services, applications, or information assets.

## Access Control Principles

The organization shall apply the following principles when granting and managing access:

- Access shall be granted based on approved business requirements.
- Users shall receive only the minimum permissions necessary to perform their assigned responsibilities (Principle of Least Privilege).
- Access requests shall require management approval before implementation.
- User permissions shall be reviewed regularly to ensure continued business justification.
- Segregation of duties shall be maintained where practical to reduce operational risk.

## User Account Management

- Individual user accounts are required for all personnel.
- Shared accounts are prohibited unless formally approved for operational purposes.
- User accounts shall be created, modified, and removed through the organization's approved access management process.
- Accounts belonging to departing employees or contractors shall be disabled promptly following termination or role changes.

## Privileged Access

Administrative and privileged accounts require additional protection due to their elevated permissions.

The organization shall:

- Require management approval before granting privileged access.
- Review privileged accounts at least quarterly.
- Log and monitor privileged activities.
- Remove temporary administrative access immediately after approved work is completed.

## Authentication Requirements

The organization shall implement secure authentication practices by:

- Requiring strong passwords for all user accounts.
- Enabling multi-factor authentication (MFA) for business-critical systems and privileged accounts.
- Changing all default passwords before systems are placed into production.
- Periodically reviewing authentication mechanisms to ensure continued effectiveness.

## Monitoring and Review

Authentication events, privileged activities, and access-related changes shall be logged and reviewed regularly. Department managers and the Information Security Manager shall conduct periodic access reviews to verify that user permissions remain appropriate.
